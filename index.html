About Me

Interdisciplinary Researcher
in AI & Technology

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow working on the UKRI-funded SMART SIP+ project at Birmingham City University, where I conduct interdisciplinary research on technology adoption, digital transformation, organisational capability development, and responsible innovation.

I hold a PhD in Technology Management from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Delhi. My doctoral research laid the foundation for my ongoing work at the intersection of organisational technological capability development, emerging technologies, and policy interventions.

My PhD research investigated how small organisations responsibly design, adopt, govern, and scale Artificial Intelligence technology while balancing innovation, business value, ethics, and regulatory compliance. I work with stakeholders across healthcare, finance, energy, and the public sector to build frameworks that make AI adoption both effective and accountable.

Expertise

Research Expertise

Six interconnected domains forming a cohesive research programme on the responsible adoption of AI across organisations and policy environments.

Artificial Intelligence

Investigating the design, deployment, and societal implications of AI systems across industries — from healthcare diagnostics to public-sector decision support.

Responsible AI

Developing evaluation criteria and maturity frameworks that help organisations embed fairness, transparency, accountability, and safety into AI systems by design.

AI Governance

Examining regulatory landscapes, corporate governance structures, and international policy frameworks that shape how AI is developed and controlled.

Technology Management

Studying how organisations strategically manage the acquisition, implementation, and value realisation of emerging digital technologies.

Business Analytics

Applying data analytics and machine learning to understand customer behaviour, decision-making, and personalisation at scale — with ethical guardrails.

Digital Transformation

Exploring the organisational, cultural, and strategic dimensions of digital change — including workforce readiness, capability development, and change management.

Featured Research

Core Research Themes

An integrated body of work examining how organisations navigate the governance, capability, and impact dimensions of AI adoption.

01
Ethics & Policy

Responsible AI

Establishing principled frameworks for trustworthy and inclusive AI that go beyond compliance — embedding ethics, fairness, and accountability at every stage of the AI lifecycle, from data curation through deployment and audit.

02
Organisational

AI Capability Assessment

Designing diagnostic tools and maturity models that help organisations accurately self-assess their technical, human, and governance readiness to deploy AI responsibly and at scale.

03
Evaluation

Technology Impact Assessment

Developing rigorous methodologies for measuring the business, social, and environmental impact of technology adoption — helping organisations justify investment and demonstrate value to stakeholders.

04
Future of Work

Human–AI Collaboration

Investigating how people and AI systems can work together effectively — exploring trust calibration, cognitive load, decision authority, and the workforce implications of augmented intelligence.

Active
UKRI · SMART SIP+

SMART SIP+ - Identifying Sustainable Business Models

SMART-SIP+' is a £2.6m project that aims to transform the lives of farmers in rural Bangladesh by advancing clean energy and sustainable agriculture led by a team of researchers from Birmingham City University in partnership with Bangladesh Agricultural University and NGOs (Bright Green Energy Foundation , Water & Sanitation for Urban Populations). This project is funded by UK Research and Innovation’s Ayrton Fund which aims to give developing countries access to the latest cutting-edge tech for reducing their emissions and meeting global climate change targets.
